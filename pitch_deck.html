The Problem

Three structural failures. One broken system.

01 · Broken signals

Credentials don't translate

A secondary certificate tells an employer almost nothing meaningful. Informal skills are entirely invisible to the formal economy.

02 · AI disruption

Automation without readiness

Jobs held by young LMIC workers face the highest displacement risk. Youth have no tools to understand or navigate this shift.

03 · No matching infrastructure

Connective tissue is absent

Even where skills and jobs coexist, matching happens through informal networks that exclude the most vulnerable youth.

"To the formal economy, Amara is unmapped. She is not the exception — she is the rule."

The Solution

Infrastructure, not just an app.

UNMAPPED is a config-driven skills infrastructure layer — any government, NGO, training provider, or employer can plug into it. Country parameters are inputs, not assumptions.

◎
Module 01

Skills Signal Engine

Maps informal experience to ISCO-08 portable skills profile. Human-readable. Explainable. Owned by Amara — not just an AI.

◉
Module 02

AI Readiness Lens

Honest automation risk assessment. Frey-Osborne scores, LMIC-adjusted. Shows durable vs. exposed skills. Calibrated per country context.

◈
Module 03

Opportunity Matching

3 realistic, grounded pathways — not aspirational. Surfaces 2+ visible econometric signals per user. Dual interface: youth + policymaker.

Our Edge

Explainability. Localizability. Realism.

What we do differently
✓
Show the data. Every score links to a named dataset the user can see.
✓
Design for constraint. Low bandwidth. Shared devices. Incomplete credentials.
✓
Localizability with evidence. Live country switch in the demo — same code, different outputs.
✓
Honest about limits. Matching is grounded, not aspirational. Risk is calibrated, not generic.
What we deliberately didn't build
✗ Full country data ingestion pipeline
✗ Complex employer marketplace
✗ Live labor-market API scraping
✗ Multi-role government admin dashboard
These are Sprint 2. A tight, explainable prototype is more persuasive than a broad, shallow platform.

Demo

Four questions. One demo. Clear answers.

Question 1

What skills does this person really have?

→ Skills Signal Engine maps informal experience to ISCO-08. Amara sees a human-readable portable profile she can own.

Question 2

What is at risk from automation?

→ AI Readiness Lens shows durable vs. exposed skills. Frey-Osborne, calibrated for Ghana. Honest. Specific. Actionable.

Question 3

What opportunities are realistic now?

→ 3 grounded pathways, each with a visible econometric signal, a fit score, and a concrete next step.

Question 4

How does this change by country?

→ Live switch from Ghana → Bangladesh → Nigeria. Same code. Different country pack. All outputs shift in real time.

If the demo answers those four questions clearly — it is a strong hackathon submission.
